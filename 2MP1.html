All about Physiotherapy & Treatment

Physiotherapy, also called physical therapy makes use of biomechanics, kinesiology, and manual therapy that helps in increasing a patient’s physical strength. It is a medical treatment that involves restoring the patient’s ability to move and function well. 

Who is a Physiotherapist?

The healthcare professionals who provide physiotherapy are called physiotherapists. They are highly trained with a degree in physiotherapy and are skilled at pinpointing the root causes of an injury, as well as treating them. He helps overcome muscle & joint pains.

Physiotherapists employ a variety of techniques such as body manipulation, electrical nerve stimulation, and Acupuncture, depending on the nature of the injury.


Types of Physio treatments:


Manipulation:

Treatment involves manually moving joints in specific directions and at different speeds to regain movement, muscle stretching, or moving the body part against resistance. It is the most effective form of physiotherapy, and it’s suitable for patients of all ages. The benefits of this treatment include improvement in blood circulation, enhanced movement and mobility. This treatment is best for aches and pains that involve the neck, shoulder, and back disorders.

Physio Exercise:

Exercises are centred on making your muscles and joints strong, improving control over your motor functions, and also preventing injuries in the future. For instance, a combination of mild exercises like walking or swimming is recommended to someone who is recovering from an injury that affects their overall mobility

Energy therapy:

This is also called electrotherapy and uses currents and pulses to stimulate your nerves. The impulses that are passed through the muscles make them tight and eases the pain and stress as well. Electrotherapy is pain-free, and you only feel a slight tingling sensation. The various types of energy therapy, include:

  • Laser therapy: This therapy aims at reducing pain and muscle spasms. Also called as, shortwave diathermy, this treatment generates an electromagnetic field that produces heat in the body’s tissues. This helps reduce any swelling and pain.
  • Ultrasound: High-frequency sound waves are used to treat any deep tissue problems. This is done by stimulating blood circulation and enhancing cell activity.
    Hydrotherapy: This form of treatment is done in water, and the temperature is between warm to hot because it lets the muscles relax and also provides relief from pain.

Problems that require Physio treatment

The need for Physio can be unpredictable, and it can come up at any age, especially if you have experienced any physical trauma like sprains, fractures or muscle disorders. A physio can help you with:

  • Posture problems : Lower and upper back pain, Stiff neck, Shoulder tightness, Poor muscle tone, Lack of balance in muscles
  • Joint pain:  Poorly aligned joints, Arthritis, Age-related joint pain, Bursitis
  • Surgery recovery: Knee replacement, Tendon surgery, Lymph node replacement, Spinal cord injury surgery
  • Bruising or Inflammation: Sports injury, Contusions, Muscle swelling, & Lymphatic congestion
  • Other: Frozen shoulder, Slip disc, Stroke, Pregnancy-related pain in muscles and joints & Cerebral Palsy
